My name is Opeyemi Akinremi. I'm the founder of The Giant Reach — a client acquisition systems agency based in Lagos, Nigeria, serving immigration and visa service businesses globally.

"Most immigration businesses don't have a marketing problem. They have a system problem. The enquiries exist — they're just leaking out at every stage."

Why Immigration Businesses Specifically

I chose to specialise in immigration and visa service businesses for a specific reason: the stakes are exceptionally high on both sides of the transaction.

A family trusting an immigration consultancy is placing their future in that business's hands. And an immigration consultancy that loses a serious applicant due to a broken follow-up system — or can't generate consistent enquiries because their ads have no landing page — is failing both itself and the people it could be helping.

I understand that personally. And I built The Giant Reach's system specifically to solve the acquisition problem that immigration businesses face — not a generic B2B system retooled for immigration, but a system designed from the ground up for this niche.

How I Diagnose the Problem (The Free Audit)

Every engagement at The Giant Reach begins with a free 15-minute acquisition audit. Before I build anything, I need to understand where the current setup is breaking down.

In that 15 minutes, I look at six things:

  • Where is traffic currently coming from — and is any of it paid?
  • Where does paid traffic land — homepage or dedicated pages?
  • How are enquiries currently captured — form, WhatsApp, phone?
  • Is there a CRM tracking applicants from first contact to consultation?
  • Is there any follow-up automation in place?
  • Is GA4 or any conversion tracking installed and measuring actual leads?

In almost every audit I run for an immigration business, I find the same pattern: there's some traffic, there are some enquiries coming in, but there's no system connecting them. No dedicated landing pages. No CRM. No automation. No tracking. Just ads running into a website — and a team manually handling whatever enquiries happen to come through.

That is the problem I solve.

How I Build the System — Step by Step

Step 1 — Traffic: Google Ads & Meta Ads for Immigration Intent

The foundation of the system is traffic — but not just any traffic. Immigration businesses need enquiries from people who are actively, seriously considering making a move. That requires intent-based keyword targeting on Google Ads and audience-based targeting on Meta.

For a UK immigration consultancy, that means keywords like "UK visa consultant," "skilled worker visa UK," "immigration lawyer London." For a Nigerian visa agency helping clients relocate to Canada, it means "Canada PR application Nigeria," "Canadian immigration consultant Lagos." Every market is different — and the campaign structure reflects that.

I structure every campaign around buyer intent — not impressions, not brand awareness. The goal is enquiries from serious applicants, not clicks from curious browsers.

Step 2 — Conversion: Dedicated Immigration Landing Pages

Traffic without conversion is money wasted. Every campaign I build is matched to a dedicated landing page — not a homepage, not a services page — a single page built specifically to convert an immigration enquiry.

That means: one headline that mirrors the ad, one clear value proposition, social proof where available, and one CTA — whether that's a WhatsApp link, a Calendly booking form, or a short enquiry form. No navigation bar. No links to other pages. No distractions. The visitor arrived with intent. The page has one job.

Step 3 — Tracking: GA4 Across All Channels

One of the most common problems I see in immigration businesses that have run ads before is that they have no idea what worked. They spent money, got some enquiries, but couldn't attribute them to specific campaigns, keywords, or platforms.

I set up GA4 with conversion events tracking every enquiry channel — WhatsApp link clicks, form submissions, Calendly bookings, phone call clicks. This means every lead is traceable back to the specific ad that generated it. The business always knows its cost per enquiry, its best-performing campaigns, and where to put more budget.

Step 4 — Pipeline: Notion CRM for Applicant Management

Leads need a home. Without a structured pipeline, enquiries exist across WhatsApp chats, email threads, sticky notes, and someone's memory — and serious applicants fall through the cracks while the team is focused on active cases.

I build a structured Notion CRM pipeline for every immigration business I work with. Every enquiry enters the pipeline at the same stage and moves through defined stages — from first contact to consultation booked to retainer signed. Every applicant is visible. Every team member knows where each prospect stands.

Step 5 — Follow-Up: Automation via Make / Pipedream

Immigration businesses lose a significant number of serious applicants not because those applicants went elsewhere — but because the follow-up was too slow. An applicant who fills in a form at 9pm on a Sunday and receives a response on Monday afternoon has often already contacted two or three other consultancies.

I build automated follow-up workflows that respond immediately — regardless of when the enquiry comes in. Using Make and Pipedream, I connect lead capture forms to WhatsApp messages, email sequences, and CRM updates. The applicant hears from the business within minutes of enquiring. Every time.

Step 6 — Optimisation: Monthly Reporting & System Improvements

The system doesn't sit still. Every month, I review the data from tracking, identify what's performing and what isn't, and make improvements to campaigns, landing pages, and follow-up sequences. The cost per enquiry goes down. The quality of applicants goes up. The system compounds over time.

Every client receives a monthly performance report — leads generated, cost per lead, pipeline value, optimisations made. No vanity metrics. Just the numbers that matter.

"The system is not set-and-forget. It improves every month because everything is measured. That is the only way acquisition compounds."

At The Giant Reach, I own the entire pipeline — traffic, conversion, tracking, CRM, automation, and optimisation — under one retainer. That means accountability at every stage, not just at the top of the funnel.
